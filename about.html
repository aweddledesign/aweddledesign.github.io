My name is Anna Weddle.

I am a freelance designer, illustrator, and photographer currently bouncing between Indianapolis and Muncie, Indiana. For the past few years, I've worked with clients in a very hands-on work environment. The Digital Corps helped me work in a collaborative team with people from many different disciplines such as development and video, helped me establish good design habits and gave me real world experience while in college.

Once I graduate from Ball State University in 2018, I have got my sights set on working for a design studio. I have flirted with the idea of settling anywhere from New York to Alaska to Australia.

So far, my portfolio includes illustration and coding work to create an educational app used by thousands of students in Indiana to celebrate the state's bicenntennial, branding work for Turner Classic Movies, a band, and a successful personal project Funcie in which I created a logo for my town and successfully marketed it as merchandise and a Snapchat geofilter, earning me Millenial cred. I have also designed multiple apps and websites as well as countless web and print materials for Ball State University as well as rebranding for various University offices.

I want to continue designing work in the public sphere and working to improve the environment I live in through design.

Thank you for checking out my work, I would love to hear from you! I am always accepting freelance and looking for new opportunities.
